Accessories

Accessories

Ties
  • **Silk ties** in classic patterns (stripes, polka dots, or small prints) or solid colors are ideal. Avoid loud or trendy prints. Stick to conservative colors like **navy, burgundy, or dark green**.
  • Bow Ties: For formal evening events, a **black or midnight blue bow tie** pairs perfectly with a tuxedo. Always opt for **self-tie** over pre-tied.
Pocket Squares
  • Pocket squares are an essential accessory for adding a touch of personality to your suit. Stick to **solid colors** or subtle patterns. **White, navy, or light blue** are versatile choices that will complement most suits.
Belts
  • A simple **leather belt in black or dark brown** is a must. Avoid large or ornate belt buckles. The belt should **match your shoes** for a cohesive look.
Watches
  • Classic timepieces are a symbol of understated wealth. Opt for **leather-strapped watches** in neutral tones or **metal bracelets in gold or silver**. Brands like Rolex, Patek Philippe, or vintage Cartier represent old money well, but the watch should never be flashy. The focus is on **craftsmanship**, not showing off.
Cufflinks
  • Simple, elegant cufflinks in **gold, silver, or mother-of-pearl** add a refined touch to formalwear. Avoid anything too garish or with bold logos.
Sunglasses and Other Accessories
  • Sunglasses: Opt for **classic aviators or wayfarers** in tortoiseshell or black. Avoid oversized or overly trendy designs. Brands like **Persol and Ray-Ban** are timeless.
  • Scarves: Lightweight wool or cashmere scarves in solid colors or subtle patterns can add sophistication to your look. Avoid overly bright colors or flamboyant designs.
  • Gloves: Leather gloves in black or brown are a stylish addition during colder months.
