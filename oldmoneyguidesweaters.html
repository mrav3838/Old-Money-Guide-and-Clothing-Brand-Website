Essential Knitwear

Material and Quality
  • The Old Money look prioritizes natural, high-quality fibers. Focus on **cashmere, merino wool, lambswool, or fine cotton** knits.
  • Avoid synthetics or overly bulky, cheaply made sweaters. The fabric should feel luxurious and drape well.
Styles and Cuts
  • **Crew Neck:** The most versatile style, perfect for wearing on its own or layering over a collared shirt.
  • **V-Neck:** Ideal for layering over a dress shirt and tie, allowing the knot to be seen.
  • **Cardigans:** Opt for fine-gauge wool or cashmere cardigans, worn buttoned up over a polo or button-up shirt.
  • **Cable Knit:** A classic preppy and "Old Money" texture, especially in white, navy, or cream.
  • **Sweater Vests:** A popular piece for layering over shirts and under blazers, particularly in diamond or subtle checked patterns.
Color Palette
  • Stick to timeless, muted colors: **navy, charcoal grey, camel/tan, cream, or burgundy**.
  • Patterns should be classic and subtle, such as thin stripes, argyle, or a simple cable weave. Avoid loud logos or complex prints.
Layering
  • Layering is key: sweaters should fit well enough to be worn under a sport coat or blazer without adding bulk.
  • A crew neck or V-neck sweater over a button-up shirt with the collar peeking out is the quintessential "Old Money" casual look.
