Suits and Formal Wear

Fabrics and Tailoring
  • Material: Old Money suiting is about using natural, luxurious fabrics. Choose suits in materials like **wool, cashmere, or a wool-silk blend**. **Linen suits** are perfect for summer.
  • Colors: Stick to a neutral palette: **navy, charcoal grey, light grey, and dark brown**. These shades exude maturity and can be worn on various formal or semi-formal occasions.
  • Cut and Fit: A well-tailored, **classic fit** is essential. Suits should be cut in a way that enhances your natural shape without being too tight or baggy. Avoid modern skinny fits or overly loose tailoring. A structured, but not stiff, silhouette is key to achieving that timeless look.
Double-Breasted or Single-Breasted?
  • Both **double-breasted** and **single-breasted** suits are acceptable, but the former often exudes more confidence and authority. The single-breasted suit, however, is versatile and more understated.
Lapels
  • Opt for **classic notched or peaked lapels**. Wider lapels add a more vintage flair, while narrower lapels tend to feel more contemporary but still within the Old Money aesthetic.
Occasions
  • For formal events, a **navy or charcoal wool suit** paired with a crisp white dress shirt and silk tie is essential.
  • A tuxedo (or black tie attire) may be required for black-tie events. In this case, a **black or deep midnight blue dinner jacket with satin lapels**, worn with a classic white tuxedo shirt and bowtie, is the epitome of sophistication.
Blazers and Sport Coats
  • Invest in high-quality blazers or sport coats in neutral colors, such as **navy, grey, or olive**. Blazers in **tweed, herringbone, or a lightweight wool** are versatile choices for semi-formal occasions. They should fit well but remain understated.
