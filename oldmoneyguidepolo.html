Shirts

Fabric and Color
  • Material: Stick to fine cotton, Egyptian cotton, or cotton blends. Poplin or Oxford cloth are two good choices for formal or casual dress shirts, respectively.
  • Colors: The Old Money wardrobe thrives on simplicity. White, light blue, and subtle pinstripes or checks are key. Avoid bold patterns or anything too trendy.
Collars
  • Classic spread collars or button-down collars are ideal for a refined look. Button-down collars are more casual but are perfect for preppy, old-money style in casual settings. Avoid extreme collar styles such as very narrow or overly wide spread collars.
Knitwear
  • Cashmere or fine wool sweaters are a key component of the Old Money wardrobe. V-neck or crew neck sweaters in solid colors (navy, grey, camel) are perfect for layering over a dress shirt.
  • For a more preppy touch, opt for a cable-knit sweater or a cashmere cardigan.
Polo Shirts
  • A classic **pique polo in navy, white, or light blue** exudes understated wealth. Brands like **Lacoste, Ralph Lauren, or Brooks Brothers** have a heritage appeal that fits within the Old Money aesthetic.
